5 Best AI Tools to Turn Videos Into Anime in 2026

If you want to turn real-life video into anime-style clips in 2026, you basically have two ways to do it:

  1. Video-to-video style transfer
    You already have footage and you just want to put an anime / cartoon skin on top while keeping the original motion.

  2. Generative video
    You start from text or images and let AI create the video from scratch. Looks cool and cinematic, but not always faithful to your original shot.

This guide is written from a very practical angle:

  • Does it look good most of the time?
  • Can a beginner use it without rage-quitting?
  • How much control do you get?
  • How fast does it render?
  • Can you roughly predict your costs?

1) LensGo AI – Best All-Rounder for Beginners

LensGo AI

If your main goal is:

“I have a normal video and I want it to look like anime, without spending all day figuring out settings.”

LensGo AI is the easiest place to start. It feels less like a “one-click filter” and more like a tiny studio in your browser.

What LensGo AI is good at

LensGo covers a few core jobs:

  • Text-to-video – type a prompt, get a short clip
  • Animate existing video – upload a clip and guide the look with text
  • Video style transfer (video → video)
    • Use anime / cartoon / 3D presets
    • Or use an image as a style reference
  • Image-to-video – take a still image / keyframe and turn it into a short animation

In plain English:

It can create videos from scratch and restyle videos you already have.

A simple beginner workflow you can copy

You can literally do this today:

  1. Start with a short, stable clip

    • Think 10 seconds or less
    • Less shake, less blur = better AI results

  2. Pick a style / model first

    • Decide: anime? cartoon? 3D? watercolor?
    • Then write your prompt. Don’t overthink it.

  3. Write a prompt that describes the look, not a novel
    Example:

    “Close-up of a skateboarder riding downhill, dynamic anime shading, clean lineart, bright colors, slight handheld camera.”

  4. Use style transfer when you care about accuracy

    • If you really want to keep the original motion and framing, use style transfer instead of pure text-to-video.

  5. Preview, then do the full render

    • Test a style
    • Adjust style strength (lighter = closer to original, stronger = more “redraw”)
    • Then render the final version

LensGo AI – Pros

  • 🧠 Very beginner-friendly

    • The UI is organized by what you want to do:
      • Generate new video
      • Turn video into anime
      • Restyle video
      • Turn image into video

  • 🎚️ Enough control without being scary

    • Style intensity
    • Apply style to whole video or characters only
    • When you pick a preset, you can still see and edit the underlying prompt

  • 🔁 Fast feedback loop

    • Generate → look at it → tweak → regenerate
    • That loop is quick, so you’re encouraged to experiment.

  • 💳 Token system is friendly for “just testing”

    • Free tokens + daily refresh = you can try stuff before paying

LensGo AI – Cons / things to be aware of

  • ⏱️ Short clips work best

    • This is true for almost every AI anime tool right now
    • LensGo is designed mainly around short clips, not 5-minute episodes

  • 🫠 Artifacts will happen

    • Fast motion, motion blur, very dark scenes, busy patterns = more risk of:
      • Weird lines
      • Flickering edges
      • Warped shapes
        This is an AI limitation in general, not just LensGo.

Tips to get better anime results with LensGo

  • Try to stabilize your footage (built-in phone stabilization is already helpful)
  • Avoid insane whip pans and very fast camera moves
  • In the prompt, focus on render style, not story:
    • ✅ “clean lineart, cel shading, limited color palette, sharp edges”
    • ❌ “like my favorite childhood anime show”
  • Use “characters only” styling when the background matters (text, UI, signs, products)
  • Pick a house style and stick to it:
    • Same style and color mood across all videos makes your channel look serious and intentional

Who should pick LensGo AI?

LensGo AI is a great fit if you’re:

  • A short-form creator (TikTok / Shorts / Reels) who wants fast anime-style clips
  • A marketer who needs stylized ads without hiring an animation studio
  • Someone who wants:
    • One tool for text-to-video, video-to-anime, image-to-video
    • A simple UI
    • Results that are “good enough to post” without hours of setup

2) Runway – Best for “Serious” Workflows

Runway is more like a full-blown AI video production tool than a simple “cool effect app”.

You should think of it as:

“I want to generate, edit, refine, and finish videos all in one place.”

Pros

  • Lots of control and features
  • Good for multi-step workflows:
    • Generate a base
    • Edit timing and scenes
    • Add extra elements, text, effects, etc.

Cons

  • More features = more time to learn
  • If you’re doing tons of iterations, it can get more expensive than lighter tools

Best for

  • Creators who care about precision and editing control
  • Teams working on commercial / client projects and want a more traditional production setup

3) Pika – Best for Fun, Fast Shorts

Pika is great if you just want to play:

  • Try wild ideas
  • Make short, eye-catching clips
  • Post a lot on social media

Pros

  • Super fast iteration
  • Outputs naturally fit short-form platforms (TikTok, Reels, etc.)

Cons

  • Not always the best when you need a perfect match to your original footage
  • For more “serious” work, you might still need another tool for polishing

Best for

  • Creators who love experimenting with styles and effects
  • People making meme videos, visual experiments, or concept tests

4) Kaiber – Best for Music-Based Edits

Kaiber shines when your video is built around music:

  • A song
  • A beat
  • A mood-driven track

You give it your audio and visuals, and it helps sync everything into something stylish and rhythm-aware.

Pros

  • Strong sense of “vibe” and motion
  • Great for:
    • Music videos
    • AMVs
    • Trailers
    • Mood edits

Cons

  • If you only want clean “video → anime” with high accuracy, it’s not always the most direct path
  • Often works best as part of a bigger workflow (Kaiber for vibe, another app for polishing)

Best for

  • Musicians, producers, DJs
  • Creators who want visuals that feel tied to their music

5) GoEnhance – Simple Video Style Transfer

GoEnhance focuses on one main thing:

“I have a clip, please restyle it.”

Its Video Style Transfer is very preset-driven and beginner-friendly.

Pros

  • Lots of presets:
    • Anime
    • Pixar-ish
    • Clay
    • Pop-art
    • etc.
  • You don’t have to understand prompts or settings too deeply

Cons

  • Presets are fun, but not super flexible
    When you want a very specific art direction, you might hit a ceiling
  • Complex scenes (lots of objects and movement) can still produce odd artifacts

Best for

  • People who just want to see “what this video looks like in different styles”
  • Users who don’t care about a full pro workflow and just want to upload → click → download

How to choose – 4 simple questions

Ask yourself these:

  1. Do you already have footage?

    • Yes → Start with LensGo AI (style transfer)
    • No → Look at Pika or Runway for generative clips

  2. Do you need strong editing control inside the same tool?

    • Yes → Runway
    • No → LensGo AI / Pika should be enough

  3. Is your video centered around music?

    • Yes → Kaiber is worth a try

  4. Do you want consistent style across many posts?

    • Yes → Use LensGo AI with:
      • One chosen style
      • A reusable prompt template
    • No → Mix and match tools and have fun

A simple starter setup (for true beginners)

If you’re just getting started and want to publish solid anime-style content without going crazy:

  • Use LensGo AI as your main engine:

    • Video → anime
    • Style transfer
    • Image → video

  • Use a basic editor on top (CapCut / Premiere / Final Cut / DaVinci Resolve):

    • Trim your video
    • Add music
    • Add captions and simple transitions
